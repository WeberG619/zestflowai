Everyone Deserves Access

We're committed to making ZestFlow AI accessible to all contractors, regardless of ability.

ZestFlow AI is committed to ensuring digital accessibility for people with disabilities. We are continually improving the user experience for everyone and applying the relevant accessibility standards.

4. Technical Implementation

4.1 Web Platform

  • Semantic HTML5 markup for proper structure
  • ARIA labels and landmarks where appropriate
  • Color contrast ratio of at least 4.5:1 for normal text
  • Focus indicators visible on all interactive elements
  • Skip navigation links at the beginning of pages
  • Descriptive page titles and headings

4.2 SMS System

  • Plain text messages compatible with screen readers
  • No reliance on color alone to convey information
  • Clear, simple language in all automated messages
  • Option to receive information via phone call instead

4.3 Dashboard & Admin Panel

  • Keyboard shortcuts for common actions
  • Screen reader announcements for dynamic content
  • Data tables with proper headers and relationships
  • Form labels and error messages clearly associated

5. Testing & Validation

We regularly test our accessibility using:

  • Automated Testing: axe DevTools, WAVE, and Lighthouse
  • Manual Testing: Keyboard navigation and screen reader testing
  • User Testing: Feedback from users with disabilities
  • Expert Review: Annual third-party accessibility audits

6. Known Issues & Workarounds

We are aware of the following accessibility issues and are working to address them:

  • Issue: Some older PDF downloads may not be fully accessible
    Workaround: Contact us for alternative formats
  • Issue: Video captions may have slight timing delays
    Workaround: Full transcripts available below each video
  • Issue: Complex data visualizations may be difficult for screen readers
    Workaround: Data tables provided as alternatives

7. Third-Party Content

While we strive to ensure all content on our platform is accessible, some third-party integrations may have limitations:

  • Payment processors (Stripe) - Generally accessible but controlled by third party
  • Analytics tools - Do not affect user-facing accessibility
  • Embedded maps - Alternative text directions provided
